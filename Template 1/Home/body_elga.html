Expected Lasallian Graduate Attributes

The Expected Lasallian Graduate Attributes (ELGA's) focus on the knowledge, skills and attributes that graduates should acquire and demonstrate in their course of studies and internships as evidence of accomplishing the school’s vision-mission. These ELGAs also reflected the graduate’s capacity for lifelong learning and transfer of knowledge in the workplace.

Critical and Creative Thinker

  • Generates ideas with resourcefulness, originality, and insight.
  • Responds to multiple experiences and ideas.
  • Uses innovative problem solving skills and methods.
  • Constructs and applies knowledge to make meaning and communicate ideas.

Effective Communcicator

  • Communicates effectively in a range of context.
  • Listens actively and responds appropraitely.
  • Communicates n various forms (oral, written, visual).
  • Express ideas critically and clearly for a variety of purposes.

Reflective Lifelong Learner

  • Critically reflects on problems and issues to shape ideas.
  • Critically evaluates on assumptions and values.
  • Plans, organizes, manages, and evaluates own thinking.
  • Develops awareness of spiritual nature and religious significance of life.

Service-Driven Citizen

  • Creates products that achieve their purpose and appropriate audience.
  • Develops and incorporates Christian leadership skills.
  • Develops and practices interpersonal skills.
  • Evaluates moral and ethical responsibilities.
